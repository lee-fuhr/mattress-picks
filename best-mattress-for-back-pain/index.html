Frequently asked questions

What mattress firmness helps with back pain?

Medium-firm is the most research-supported firmness for back pain, it provides lumbar support without creating pressure points at the hips and shoulders. Very soft mattresses let the hips sag out of alignment; very firm ones push back against natural spine curvature.

Can the wrong mattress cause back pain?

Yes. A mattress that's too soft allows the lower back to sink into a curved position overnight, which builds cumulative strain. Too firm creates pressure at the hips that causes compensatory spinal misalignment. Waking up stiffer than when you went to bed is the key signal.

Is a firm or soft mattress better for lower back pain?

Medium-firm, with nuance by sleep position. Back sleepers with lower back pain typically need more firmness for lumbar support. Side sleepers with back pain often need medium softness to relieve hip and shoulder pressure that would otherwise pull the spine out of alignment.

How long does it take to tell if a mattress helps back pain?

At least 3-4 weeks of consistent sleep. Initial adjustment discomfort is normal as your body adapts. Most back pain improvement from the right mattress shows up in the 4-8 week range, why a 100+ night trial period matters.

Does sleeping position matter more than mattress firmness for back pain?

Both matter, but position and firmness interact. The best mattress for your back pain depends heavily on whether you're a back, side, or stomach sleeper, there's no single universal answer, which is why matching firmness to position is essential.
