Side sleepers face a unique challenge: their body weight concentrates on the hips and shoulders, requiring a mattress that cushions these pressure points without allowing the spine to curve out of alignment. The wrong mattress can lead to numbness, shoulder pain, or restless tossing. A mattress for side sleepers should provide deep contouring in the top layers while maintaining enough core support to keep the hips from sinking too far. The key factors to weigh are firmness (medium or soft is usually best), material (memory foam or hybrid for contouring), and temperature regulation. Many side sleepers also benefit from zoned support, which firms up the center of the mattress to keep the spine neutral. Frequently asked questions

What firmness is best for side sleepers?

Medium to medium-soft. Side sleeping puts pressure on the shoulder and hip, a softer surface allows these areas to sink in while keeping the spine aligned. Too firm creates pressure points; too soft lets the hips drop too far.

Can side sleepers use a firm mattress?

Generally not without discomfort. Firm mattresses push back against the shoulder and hip rather than conforming, which creates pressure points and often leads to arm numbness or hip soreness. Stomach sleepers and heavier back sleepers are better served by firm.

What is zoned support and does it help side sleepers?

Zoned support uses different firmness in different regions, softer at the shoulders, firmer at the hips and core. For side sleepers it can meaningfully improve both pressure relief and spinal alignment. The Helix Midnight uses this approach effectively.

Do side sleepers need a thicker mattress?

Thicker helps but isn't the whole answer. A 10-12 inch mattress with proper foam layering achieves better pressure relief than an 8-inch mattress. Thickness matters most for heavier sleepers who compress the top layers more deeply.

Is memory foam or hybrid better for side sleepers?

Both can work. Memory foam excels at conforming to the shoulder and hip. Hybrid adds responsiveness and cooler sleep. If you sleep hot or move a lot at night, a hybrid is usually the better choice.
