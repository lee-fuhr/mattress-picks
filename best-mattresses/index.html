Frequently asked questions

How firm should a mattress be?

It depends on sleep position. Side sleepers need medium to medium-soft to relieve shoulder and hip pressure. Back sleepers do best with medium-firm for lumbar support. Stomach sleepers need firm to prevent lower-back hyperextension. Combination sleepers generally do well with medium.

How long should a mattress last?

A quality mattress typically lasts 7-10 years. Signs it needs replacing: visible sagging (even 1-1.5 inches affects sleep quality), waking up stiffer than before, or consistently sleeping better elsewhere. Most warranties cover manufacturing defects, not wear-related sagging.

What is the best mattress trial policy?

100 nights is the minimum meaningful trial, it takes 3-4 weeks to adjust to a new mattress surface and another few weeks to assess real comfort. Nectar offers 365 nights. Most reputable brands include free returns during the trial period.

Are hybrid mattresses worth the premium over all-foam?

For most sleepers, yes. Coils improve edge support, sleep cooler than foam, and respond faster for combination sleepers who move during the night. All-foam excels at motion isolation and tends to cost less. The right choice depends on your priorities.

Does mattress weight matter for setup?

Mattresses in a box are compressed for shipping and expand within hours of unpacking, easier to handle than traditional delivery. White-glove service (Saatva, WinkBed) handles setup entirely, worth considering if hauling a heavy mattress upstairs is a concern.
